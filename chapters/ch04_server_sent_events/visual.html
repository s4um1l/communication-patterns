What to watch for: SSE (left) maintains a single persistent connection line. Events flow down it as colored blobs -- no reconnection between events. Long polling (right inset) must reconnect after every single event, adding ~900 bytes of HTTP overhead each time. During a burst of events, SSE delivers them back-to-back instantly; long polling queues them behind reconnection delays. Watch the bandwidth counter -- the gap grows with every event.
